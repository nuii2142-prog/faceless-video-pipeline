# Zen Script Format Spec

Reverse-engineered from a real high-performer (the same method Rogoff uses in the
reference video — extract the format from what's already going viral, don't invent one).

**Source clip:** @Zenn0009 — "What Did Ancient Humans Do at Night?" — **7.7M views, 8:32 long.**
(This is the exact 7M video Rogoff analyzed on-screen.)
**Channel pattern:** every Zen video is **7–13 min long-form, 16:9 landscape**, calm
philosophical/educational explainer. NOT 90-second Shorts. (Important — see "Length variants".)

---

## The overall shape (long-form native, ~1,300 words @ ~150 wpm)

1. **HOOK** (~0:00–0:40) — 4 micro-beats
2. **BODY** — exactly **3 pillars**, each: transition → real named study → vivid development → memorable line
3. **CLOSE** — reframe + lyrical 3-beat recap + callback to the opening image

---

## 1. The hook formula (the most important 40 seconds) — 4 micro-beats

| Beat | Job | From the real clip |
|------|-----|--------------------|
| **B1 — Immersive present** | Drop the viewer INTO a familiar everyday moment, 2nd person, present/future tense | *"Tonight, when the sun goes down, you're going to flip a switch. Light will flood the room and you won't think twice about it."* |
| **B2 — Contrarian scale pivot** | "But for [huge timespan] this wasn't true" → make the ordinary feel strange | *"But for 99.9% of human history, that switch didn't exist. When the sun set... you couldn't even see your own hand in front of your face."* |
| **B3 — Raise stakes + central question** | One vivid stat, then THE question | *"For over 300,000 years, every ancestor spent half their life in near-total darkness... So what did they actually do for all those hours?"* |
| **B4 — Core promise (names the pillars)** | Promise a payoff + preview the 3 pillars | *"The answer changes everything we think we know about sleep, storytelling, and what it means to be human."* |

---

## 2. Body = 3 pillars

Each pillar repeats the same engine:
- **Transition sentence** (curiosity bridge) — see below
- **Real named evidence** — researcher + year + institution/journal + place
- **Vivid concrete development** — sensory, second-person, specific numbers
- **One memorable line** that crystallizes the pillar

Real examples of the "evidence" anchors in this clip:
- Pillar 1 (fire/storytelling): Wonderwerk Cave, South Africa, ~1M yrs ago · Polly Wiesner, 2014, PNAS · "81% of night conversations were stories" → *"the campfire was the original theater, classroom, church. It's where we became us."*
- Pillar 2 (biphasic sleep): Roger Ekirch, 2001, 16 yrs / 500+ references / Homer's Odyssey · Thomas Wehr, 1992, NIMH experiment · elevated prolactin → *"This wasn't insomnia. This was normal."*
- Pillar 3 (artificial light): Paris street lamps 1600s (Louis XIV) · Edison 1879 · "curfew = couvre-feu = cover fire" → melatonin/blue-light, camping resets cycle by 2 hrs.

### Transitions (bridge sentences — memorize these patterns)
- *"But here's what's fascinating."*
- *"Here's the part nobody talks about."*
- *"But the night wasn't just about X. There was something else happening..."*
- *"So what killed it? The answer is simpler than you'd think."*

---

## 3. Authority rules (why it feels TRUE) — your "personal + real stats" choice lives here

- Every surprising claim is anchored to a **REAL named source**: researcher + year + institution + place.
- **Specific numbers** beat vague ones: "300,000 years", "30 feet", "81%", "500 references", "1879".
- ⚠️ **Stats must be REAL.** The skill researches and cites genuine studies. NEVER fabricate a study,
  number, or researcher. A made-up citation that gets caught kills channel trust.

---

## 4. Tone & sentence craft

- **Second person throughout** — "you", "your ancestors". Pulls the viewer in.
- **Short declarative sentences.** Present tense for immediacy. Easy to read aloud (TTS / self-record).
- **Repetition for rhythm:** *"Inside that circle, predators won't come. Outside that circle, you die."*
- **Rhetorical questions** to pivot between sections.
- Calm, awe-building, a little existential. No hype, no emojis, no "subscribe" mid-script.

---

## 5. Close formula

1. **Reframe** the everyday thing the hook opened with: *"What if insomnia isn't a disorder... it's your body remembering what it was built to do?"*
2. **Lyrical 3-beat recap** of the pillars (fires & stories / two-phase sleep / total darkness).
3. **Final line = callback to the opening image** + gut-punch: *"We traded all of that for a light switch. And most of us never even knew it was gone."*

---

## 6. Length variants (THE decision that drives everything)

| | Long-form (Zen native) | Short (≤60s hard cap) |
|---|---|---|
| Length | ~8 min · ~1,300 words | 40–55 s (cap 60) · ~100–140 words |
| Structure | Hook (4 beats) + **3 pillars** + close | Hook (from the hook wheel) + **1 pillar** (1 study + 1 lived scene) + callback close — body order rotates via the structure wheel (SKILL A4) |
| Aspect | 16:9 landscape | 9:16 vertical |
| Images | ~50–90 (1 per phrase) | ~14–22 beats |
| Money | **Earns watch-hours → real YouTube monetization (what makes Zen $11.7K/mo)** | Subscriber funnel; Shorts RPM is low |
| Effort/clip | High | Low — good for validating the pipeline first |

> 💡 Honest take: Zen's money comes from **long-form**. Shorts are a funnel. A sensible path is
> 90s Shorts first to prove the pipeline + grow subs, then long-form once it works. The skill below
> supports both via a `length` setting so you don't need two skills.
